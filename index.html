About Me

I am a really cool person. I love many things, and many things love me. Because I am so loveable. One time I even loved an ant. Not that theres anything wrong with that. Right? You agree, right? Because theres not. Ants are incredible little creatures with beautiful eyes and legs. I mean, they are so strong! They can lift stuff up. So yes, I loved an ant once. Not only that, but I also once locked eyes with a cyclops. I was on the beach in Tahiti. It was dusk, the sun was setting behind the ocean and I was alone. I sat down in the sand and started making kissy faces at a crab I saw coming out of its burrow. Thats when I heard a noise like the one someone makes when theyve just stubbed their toe. I looked up and saw her. Long brown hair, she was wearing a poncho. It was beautiful. She turned to face me. I didnt mean to, but I gasped. I know she heard it. I also know she saw me making kissy faces at the crabs. I was embarassed. I wanted to cry. I wanted to make like the crabs and burrow. I would have burrowed a few feet down and then all the way back to my room. But I am no crab. Instead I just said "hi." She didnt respond. We stared into each others eyes for what felt like 5 minutes. Then I slowly got up and backed my way off the beach. She didnt move once. I made it to my room and got in to bed but could not sleep. Every time I would fall asleep, I would quickly be awoken by a horrific dream. It was the same dream every time. It was her on the beach, but it wasnt the beach. It was more like we were on the edge of a volcano. She just kept staring at me while making this high pitched noise. Like a bird. She started flapping her arms like she would fly, but she didnt fly. Instead, eventually, her legs just seemed to give out and she fell into the volcano. Thats when I wake up. I had that dream over and over. When morning finally came I went back out to the beach and she was still there. Completely motionless. So I walked up to her and asked if she was alright. No response. She didnt even blink. Her eyes fixed on a point straight ahead and off into the distance. I asked again, no response. I gave her shoulder a poke. As soon as I made contact she began gasping for air. She started crying. She told me she had been paralyzed! She said she had stood there all night trapped in her thoughts! She was shaking and crying. I asked her if I could help her but she would not look into my eyes. She was trying to keep her eyes closed so as not to look at me. She wouldnt say why but she left in a hurry. Panicked. She ran off into the distance and I never saw her again. I left Tahiti 3 days later.
